# Sensemaking: Why Was GATE Misclassified as a Protocol?

## SV6 — Stabilized Model

**The definition leaked at the word "manages."**

"Connects, configures, transfers, or manages" — the first three verbs are specific operational actions. "Manages" is vague enough to include both routing (operational — protocol territory) and quality evaluation (cognitive — discipline territory). GATE slipped through because "manages the outputs" could mean "checks if they're good enough."

### The boundary

**Protocols ROUTE. Disciplines JUDGE.**

Every surviving protocol does routing, sequencing, configuring, transferring, or persisting. None of them judge quality. GATE was the only one that tried to judge — and it was eliminated because quality evaluation is cognitive work.

### Definition change

Old: "...connects, configures, transfers, or manages the outputs..."
New: "...routes, configures, sequences, transfers, or persists the outputs and state... Protocols do not evaluate quality."

### Category change

Quality Protocols category dissolved. Contents reclassified:
- GATE → eliminated (not a protocol — quality evaluation is cognitive)
- Self-assessment, depth tests, convergence checks → discipline components (not protocols)
- Freshness checks → moved to Pipeline (precondition — genuinely operational)

Three categories remain: Pipeline, Transfer, Persistence.

### The deeper lesson

When something tries to both judge and route, it's either:
- A discipline component doing operational work (self-assessment) → lives inside the discipline
- A protocol overstepping into cognitive territory (GATE) → should be eliminated
