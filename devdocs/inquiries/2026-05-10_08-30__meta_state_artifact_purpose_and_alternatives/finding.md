# Finding: Meta-state Artifact Purpose and Alternatives

## Question

**From `_branch.md`:** What does the `_meta_state.md` artifact (introduced at Level 1 of the meta-loop level progression as a visited-path list of `(inquiry → selected direction → rationale)`) actually serve, beyond preventing double-visitation, and is there a simpler alternative that delivers the same functions without the cost of maintaining a separate cross-run state file?

**Goal:** a clear list of the functions the artifact would serve, an honest assessment of which justify the file, a candidate-set of alternatives, and a buildable recommendation. The user should be able to decide today: build `_meta_state.md`, replace it with a simpler alternative, or skip it entirely.

## Finding Summary

- **The user's instinct is structurally correct: at Level 1 (the buildable-today meta-loop), `_meta_state.md` is not necessary.** Anti-double-visitation is a thin function — it doesn't justify a new artifact on its own, and most of the artifact's other named functions are either derivable from the existing inquiry corpus or better recorded inline near the point of decision.
- **The functions split into three groups by where they naturally live.** The first group (path memory, durable cross-run memory, stateful-traversal evidence, stop/continue support, double-visitation prevention) is derivable by walking the existing inquiry corpus — `finding.md` frontmatter chains and `_branch.md` Relationships sections — without any new file. The second group (selection rationale, calibration data for future automated selectors) is recording-required, but its natural home is the next inquiry's `_branch.md` at the moment that inquiry is created. The third group (anti-spinning detection, multi-head branch/merge state) is human-handled at Level 1; spinning detection becomes a system function only at Level 4 when selection becomes autonomous.
- **The recommended Level-1 implementation is one small addition to the next inquiry's `_branch.md`** — a `SELECTED-FROM` line (or a small dedicated subsection) carrying parent path, Navigation direction, and one-line rationale. No separate cross-run state file is built.
- **The meta-loop spec's "memory" language is preserved.** The spec uses "meta-state" consistently as an abstract concept — "meta-state is its memory." The specific file name `_meta_state.md` appears only in the spec's Level-1 example flow (introduced with "Meta-loop v1 *can be*..." — one sufficient implementation, not a mandate) and in a `COULD`-tagged design action. The corpus-plus-rationale implementation honors the abstract concept (memory of the path) using a different shape than a discrete file.
- **Levels above 1 evolve in a principled way.** At Level 2 or Level 3, if walking the corpus becomes expensive, an optional generated INDEX view can summarize the path on demand — generated by a script, not maintained as a primary file. At Level 4 (multi-head autonomous selection), a real cross-head state structure may be required for branch-set membership and merge points; that decision is deferred until Level 4 is being built, with concrete requirements rather than speculation. The meta-loop spec's own Open Question — "does it need a branch graph from the beginning, or is a visited-path list enough?" — is answered: defer until evidence is available.

## Finding

This finding answers whether the meta-loop's `_meta_state.md` artifact is necessary at the buildable level (Level 1 of the level progression — sequential meta-loop with a human Selector, runnable today), and proposes a simpler implementation that delivers the same load-bearing functions without a separate cross-run state file.

### Why the artifact would exist — the function list

The meta-loop spec at `enes/loop_desing_ideas/meta_loop.md` describes the meta-loop as a "stateful traversal engine" and says "meta-state gives it memory." Reading the spec carefully reveals at least eight distinct functions the meta-state would serve:

- **Path memory** — where has the meta-loop been across prior runs.
- **Durable cross-run memory** — survives across sessions; the meta-loop has continuity, not amnesia per session.
- **Stateful-traversal evidence** — what makes the meta-loop "stateful" rather than a stateless runner.
- **Stop/continue decision support** — the runner needs context to decide whether to take another step.
- **Anti-double-visitation** — don't re-run a probe that already ran.
- **Selection rationale recording** — the WHY of each move (not just the WHAT).
- **Anti-spinning judgment substrate** — the spec calls this "tells it whether it is thinking or just spinning."
- **Branch and merge support** — multi-head meta-loops need cross-head state.
- **Calibration data** — recorded selections + outcomes that future automated selectors can train on.

### The three groups (and where each belongs at Level 1)

Sorted by where each function naturally lives in this project's existing artifact structure:

| Group | Functions in the group | Level-1 home |
|---|---|---|
| **Corpus-derivable** (no new file needed) | path memory, durable cross-run memory, stateful-traversal evidence, stop/continue support, anti-double-visitation | walk existing `finding.md` frontmatter chains (`refines:` / `supersedes:` / `corrects:`) and `_branch.md` Relationships sections |
| **Recording-required at point-of-use** (one new field, in an existing artifact) | selection rationale, calibration data accumulation | a small addition to the next inquiry's `_branch.md` at the moment that inquiry is created |
| **Human-handled at Level 1** (system function only at higher levels) | anti-spinning detection, multi-head branch/merge state | the human Selector notices repetition and avoids it; multi-head doesn't exist at Level 1 |

The first group needs nothing new. The third group is human responsibility until Level 4. The second group is the entire load-bearing addition — and its home is point-of-use, not a centralized cross-run file.

### Concrete Level-1 implementation — two options

When the meta-loop selects a Navigation direction from inquiry-X and spawns inquiry-Y, the new inquiry's `_branch.md` records the selection. Two ways to do this:

**Option A — inline as a typed Relationship (simpler; default).** Add `SELECTED-FROM` as another typed entry alongside `BUILDS-ON` / `RELATED` / `REFINES` in the existing Relationships section:

```
## Relationships

- SELECTED-FROM: devdocs/inquiries/<parent_id>/finding.md (direction:
  <Navigation type or specific direction text>) — <one-line rationale>
- BUILDS-ON: devdocs/inquiries/<parent_id>/finding.md (the prior inquiry
  this one builds content on)
- RELATED: ...
```

Pro: leverages the existing typed-Relationships pattern; one section, no new structure. Con: the meta-loop traversal record sits alongside content-relationships, and a quick read doesn't immediately distinguish them.

**Option B — separate "Selection" subsection (more explicit).** Add a small block immediately before Relationships, omitted entirely for inquiries not produced by meta-loop traversal:

```
## Selection (when produced by meta-loop traversal)

- **Parent:** devdocs/inquiries/<parent_id>/finding.md
- **Direction:** <Navigation type or specific direction text>
- **Rationale:** <one-line: why this direction over others>

## Relationships
- BUILDS-ON: ...
```

Pro: signals at a glance "this inquiry was meta-loop-spawned"; keeps traversal records structurally separate from content-relationships. Con: extra section that's empty for user-seeded inquiries.

**Recommendation: default to Option A.** It's the smaller change, leverages the existing typed-Relationships pattern, and is easy to switch to Option B later if the meta-loop adds more cross-cutting fields (branch-set membership, merge identifiers) that justify a dedicated section. Both options work; the choice can be revisited when more meta-loop-specific data accumulates.

A concrete example (using Option A) — the inquiry that produced this finding could have been spawned by the meta-loop selecting direction "DEEPEN" from the prior navigation-audit finding:

```
## Relationships

- SELECTED-FROM: devdocs/inquiries/2026-05-10_03-50__navigation_established_audit/finding.md (direction: DEEPEN — challenge the meta-loop's _meta_state.md artifact) — User instinct that the artifact may not be necessary; testing whether functions can be served without it.
- BUILDS-ON: enes/loop_desing_ideas/meta_loop.md (the meta-loop spec being investigated)
```

### Level-aware roadmap

The "drop the artifact" verdict is Level-1 specific. Higher levels evolve as follows:

| Level | What changes about the project's meta-loop | Verdict on cross-run state |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 0 (current)** | No meta-loop runner exists. The human holds traversal context in their head as they navigate inquiries manually. | No artifact needed. |
| **Level 1 (sequential, human Selector)** | Meta-loop runner exists. Human picks the next direction after each Navigation map. | Add the `SELECTED-FROM` field (Option A) to the next inquiry's `_branch.md`. No separate state file. |
| **Levels 2 and 3 (persistent or graph-aware Navigator)** | Cross-run patterns become more visible. Walking the inquiry corpus to reconstruct path memory may start costing noticeable time. | Optionally add a generated INDEX view — a script like `tools/meta_loop_index.sh` that scans the corpus and emits a flat summary on demand. The INDEX is a derived view, not a primary artifact; it can be regenerated any time. |
| **Level 4 (multi-head autonomous selection)** | Multiple heads run in parallel. Branch-set membership, merge points, and machine spin-detection all become system functions. | Design a real cross-head state structure THEN, informed by Level-1-through-3 evidence about what branch/merge state is actually needed. The meta-loop spec's own Open Question — "does it need a branch graph from the beginning, or is a visited-path list enough?" — is exactly this deferral. |

### Optional Level-2/3 INDEX sketch

If at Level 2 or 3 corpus-walk costs become noticeable, the INDEX view can be generated on demand by a small script. The script reads `_branch.md` files for the SELECTED-FROM line and emits a flat summary. Truth lives in each `_branch.md`; the INDEX is a cache.

```bash
# tools/meta_loop_index.sh — generated view, not primary state
grep -lE 'SELECTED-FROM:' devdocs/inquiries/*/_branch.md \
  | while read f; do
      inquiry=$(dirname "$f")
      sed -n '/SELECTED-FROM/p' "$f" \
        | sed "s|^|## $inquiry: |"
    done > devdocs/_meta_loop_index.md
```

Optional. Not part of the Level-1 build.

## Next Actions

### MUST

- **What:** When building the Level-1 meta-loop runner, populate a `SELECTED-FROM` line (Option A) in the next inquiry's `_branch.md` Relationships section at the moment the runner spawns the inquiry.
  - **Who:** the meta-loop runner implementation.
  - **Gate:** before the meta-loop runner ships its first usable version.
  - **Why:** captures the selection rationale near the point of decision; covers the recording-required functions without a separate state file.

- **What:** Do not design or build `_meta_state.md` v1 as a primary artifact at Level 1.
  - **Who:** anyone working on the meta-loop spec or implementation.
  - **Gate:** until evidence from Level 1 (sequential) and Levels 2-3 (persistent / graph-aware) reveals concrete requirements that the corpus + `_branch.md` field cannot serve.
  - **Why:** prevents premature commitment to a visited-path-list shape that may not match Level-4 multi-head needs; the meta-loop spec's own Open Question on visited-path-list-vs-branch-graph already flagged this uncertainty.

### COULD

- **What:** When meta-loop-specific data starts accumulating beyond the single rationale line — for example, branch-set membership at Level 4 — switch from Option A (inline `SELECTED-FROM`) to Option B (dedicated "Selection" subsection).
  - **Who:** future meta-loop spec or implementation revision.
  - **Gate:** when more than two cross-cutting fields are needed in `_branch.md` for meta-loop traversal records.
  - **Why:** separating traversal records from content-relationships becomes worth the extra section once there is more than one field.

- **What:** Build `tools/meta_loop_index.sh` as a generated view over the corpus.
  - **Who:** future tooling pass.
  - **Gate:** when meta-loop runs at Level 2 or 3 produce enough volume that walking `_branch.md` files per step becomes noticeable.
  - **Why:** keeps truth in each `_branch.md` while making the path-summary cheap to read; the INDEX is regenerated, not maintained.

### DEFERRED

- **What:** Design a real cross-head meta-state structure (branch sets, merge points, machine-readable selection records).
  - **Gate:** when multi-head autonomous meta-loop (Level 4) is being built and concrete requirements have surfaced from Levels 1-3 use.
  - **Why if revived:** Level-4 selection happens without a human and across parallel heads; structures that don't exist at Level 1 (branch-set membership, machine spin-detection) become necessary then. The L4 design should respond to actual Level-1-through-3 evidence, not speculate from the Level-1 design.

## Reasoning

**Why drop the artifact at Level 1.** Five of the eight named functions — path memory, durable cross-run memory, stateful-traversal evidence, stop/continue support, and anti-double-visitation — derive from walking the existing corpus. `finding.md` frontmatter chains, `_branch.md` Relationships sections, and the filesystem itself already encode where prior inquiries came from and what they relate to. Building a parallel `_meta_state.md` file duplicates this corpus and creates a divergence risk: the file and the corpus could disagree, and reconciling them is overhead. Two of the remaining three functions (anti-spinning, branch/merge state) are not Level-1 system functions — at Level 1 with a human Selector, the human notices spinning and there is no multi-head. Only one function — selection rationale — is genuinely new at Level 1, and its natural home is the inquiry it produced.

**Why record selection rationale at point-of-use.** The selection rationale is the one piece of information the meta-loop produces that the corpus doesn't already contain. It belongs in the next inquiry's `_branch.md` for two reasons. First, the next `_branch.md` is being created at the exact moment the selection happens — recording is local and immediate. Second, the rationale is most useful to a future reader at the inquiry it produced, not in a centralized list far from context. Calibration data for future automated selectors accumulates automatically as the rationale field populates across the corpus; an automated selector reads the rationale lines when it's eventually built.

**Why defer Level-4 cross-head state design.** The meta-loop spec's Open Questions explicitly ask whether `_meta_state.md` needs a branch graph from the beginning or whether a visited-path list is enough. The answer that emerges from this inquiry is: defer. The Level-4 multi-head shape is not knowable until at least Level 2 produces evidence about what cross-run patterns matter. Building `_meta_state.md` v1 today commits to a shape (visited-path list per the spec's own Level-1 description) that may not match Level-4's needs. The Level-1 `_branch.md` field doesn't preclude any Level-4 structure: at Level 4, additional fields can be added (e.g., `BranchSet:` for multi-head session identifier), or a separate cross-head state file can complement the per-inquiry rationale. The Level-1 choice is forward-compatible.

**Spec-compatibility.** This needs to be precise to avoid overclaiming. The meta-loop spec at `enes/loop_desing_ideas/meta_loop.md` uses "meta-state" as an abstract concept consistently — "meta-state is its memory" appears at lines 13, 45, 53, and the reasoning that "the meta-loop cannot be stateful without durable cross-run memory" appears at line 158. The specific file name `_meta_state.md` appears in two narrower contexts: the Level-1 example flow at lines 121 and 126, and a `COULD`-tagged design action at line 155. The Level-1 flow is introduced with "Meta-loop v1 *can be*: ..." (line 117) — explicitly one sufficient implementation, not a mandate. The `COULD`-tag on "Design `_meta_state.md` v1" already signals the spec author's uncertainty about whether the file was necessary at the buildable level. So this verdict preserves the abstract concept (meta-state as memory of the path) while choosing a different implementation (corpus walk plus per-inquiry `_branch.md` field). It is not stretching the spec; it is choosing one of the implementations the spec leaves open.

**Coupling note.** The meta-loop runner reads and writes the `SELECTED-FROM` field in `_branch.md`. This creates a documented coupling between the meta-loop runner and MVL+'s `_branch.md` template — if either changes incompatibly, both must update together. This is normal runner-to-artifact coupling; named here so the dependency is visible.

**Alternatives rejected.** Three alternatives were considered and rejected. Building `_meta_state.md` v1 now is premature commitment to a visited-path shape that may not survive Level-4 requirements, and it duplicates the corpus that already encodes most of what the file would carry. Replacing the artifact with a per-meta-loop-session journal (one file per session, not cross-session) loses the durable-cross-run-memory function that the spec explicitly demands. Strictly prohibiting all cross-meta-loop fields in `_branch.md` would force selection rationale to live somewhere else and would lose the point-of-use recording advantage; the alternative was rejected as over-correction.

## Open Questions

### Refinement Triggers

- **If walking the inquiry corpus becomes noticeably slow during Level-2 or Level-3 use** (more than a few seconds per meta-loop step), build the optional INDEX script. Trigger: observed corpus-walk latency.
- **If meta-loop-specific data in `_branch.md` grows beyond the single `SELECTED-FROM` line** (e.g., branch-set identifiers at Level 4), switch from Option A (inline) to Option B (dedicated Selection subsection). Trigger: more than two cross-cutting meta-loop fields needed.
- **If the user runs Level-1 meta-loop chains and notices the human Selector failing to detect spinning** (the same area being explored repeatedly without recognition), reconsider whether anti-spinning needs a system-function shape earlier than Level 4. Trigger: observed unrecognized spinning across two or more chains.

### Research Frontiers

- **Whether selection rationale at point-of-use produces good calibration data.** When eventually building an automated selector (deferred until ten Navigation maps with selections plus outcomes exist, per the meta-loop spec), confirm that rationale lines accumulated through this design are training-quality. If they prove too sparse or unstructured, a richer recording shape may be needed.

### Blocked

- **Level-4 cross-head state structure** is blocked until Level 1 ships and Levels 2-3 produce evidence about what multi-head needs.
