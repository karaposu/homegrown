**Signals detected after Cycle 1:**

- **Density (high candidates, high relevance):** R2 Dynamic-motion, R3 Composition, R5 Recursion, R6 Loop. These four regions cluster around what the project actually claims (consciousness as chained, recursive, self-modifying cognition).
- **Tension:** R1 Static-form is the rejected region (where "structure" sits) — but the colon expansion in the README ("It's the X: the loop that...") needs an X that's noun-shaped enough to be a thing. Pure-process or pure-verb phrasings ("the chaining," "the looping") risk reading as gerunds, not things.
- **Density (low):** R7 Grammar, R9 Biology — fewer candidates per region but each highly evocative.
- **Risk:** R10 Practice / Craft — "the discipline of thinking" would overload Homegrown's own term ("disciplines" is already the project's primary noun).
- **Absence:** No region directly names "the connecting tissue between cognitive operations" as a single word — every candidate borrows from somewhere else.
- **Novelty:** R9 Biology's "autopoiesis" (self-creating system, Maturana/Varela) is the most accurate technical term in cognitive science but is too obscure for a README tagline.

**Resolution decision:** Zoom in on R2, R3, R5, R6 (highest relevance). Lightly probe R7 and R9 (high-evocation, low-density). Confirm absence in R1 (rejected — already known to be rigid) and R10 (rejected — term overload).

## Jump Scan (uncharted directions)

Before declaring convergence, deliberately scan a different direction: not noun phrases describing the *missing thing*, but **subject-rephrasings** that sidestep the noun-replacement problem entirely.

| Alternative framing | Example | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Replace the noun-phrase slot with a **gerund** | "It's the **chaining**: the loop that takes a single LLM call and chains it into..." | ✓ Lighter; loses the "thinking" qualifier; risks gerund/noun confusion |
| Replace with an **adjective+noun combo** | "It's the **recursive cognition**" / "the **chained reasoning**" | ✓ Works; "recursive cognition" reads philosophical; "chained reasoning" reads engineering |
| Replace with a **negation** ("not X but Y") | "It's not raw intelligence — it's intelligence **looping back on itself**" | ✓ Works rhetorically; restructures the sentence |
| Replace with **hybrid noun phrase** | "the dynamics of recursive cognition" / "the recursive composition of thinking" | ✓ Survives if the tagline can absorb 8+ syllables (line 3 cannot; line 5 can) |

**Jump-scan finding:** The single-noun-phrase frame is not the only frame. Two more options earn a place on the map:
- **Adjective+noun**: "recursive cognition" / "chained cognition"
- **Hybrid noun phrase**: "the dynamics of recursive cognition"

## Gaps and Recommendations

**Bounded gaps remaining:**
- **R7 grammar baggage** — does "grammar of thinking" connote rigid school-grammar rules, or generative-grammar (Chomsky) infinite expression? Sensemaking should resolve this disambiguation.
- **R5 stance commitment** — does committing to "recursion" on the tagline help or hurt? Sensemaking should locate the right stance level.
- **Rhythmic-vs-semantic tradeoff** — line 3 wants ≤6 syllables; line 5 can absorb 8+. May need two different phrasings, or one phrasing that fits both. Decomposition should partition the two slots.

**Recommendation for next discipline (Sensemaking):**
The eight surviving candidates fall into three semantic clusters that need disambiguation before Innovation can recombine or refine:
- **Behavior cluster**: dynamics, composition, chaining (what cognition *does*)
- **Self-reference cluster**: recursion (what cognition *is structurally*)
- **Generativity cluster**: grammar (what cognition *enables*)

Sensemaking should clarify which of these clusters most accurately names what raw LLM use lacks — because that's the criterion the README's bet paragraph rests on.
