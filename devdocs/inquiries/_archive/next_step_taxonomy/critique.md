### Types from C's output (1c+3b+7c)
**Prosecution:** C produces ~6 types. Other 9 come from telemetry, scope check, human judgment. "No selection needed" overclaims.
**Defense:** 9 of 15 auto-derivable (C + telemetry + scope check). 6 need judgment. Significant subset, not everything.
**Verdict: REFINE** — Use the automatable/human split as primary framing. "9 auto, 6 judgment."

### R IS navigation (7b+2c)
**Prosecution (incorporating user's observation):** Reflection = backward, process, learning. Navigation = forward, content, directing. Different questions, different temporal orientations, different outputs, different purposes. Merging conflates two distinct cognitive operations. The user is structurally correct.
**Defense:** They share input (reading the SIC run). They should run at the same point (after C). N benefits from R's observations.
**Collision → Refinement:** Assessment is the COMMON operation. Two VIEWS: reflection (backward) and navigation (forward). Not merged. Not separate steps. One assessment, two outputs.

**Verdict: REFINE** — Assessment → two views. Preserves both identities.

### 15-type taxonomy
**Prosecution:** Some types may overlap (DEEPEN vs RE-RUN DEEPER, MERGE vs CONSOLIDATE).
**Defense:** Triggers and framings are genuinely different. Usage will reveal which merge.
**Verdict: SURVIVE (caveat)** — Candidate list. Monitor for merge-worthy overlaps.
