- Total deliverable is moderate, not heavy.

The phase-fit reframing doesn't conflict with the convention — it just precisifies which layer of activity ("machinery" vs. "maintenance") the descriptive-only constraint applies to.

## Phase 3 — Ambiguity Collapse

### Ambiguity 1: Is the three-form framing load-bearing?

**Strongest counter:** Form 3 has only N=1 (sense-making's structured ambiguity entry template). Naming a form from N=1 risks codifying an accidental observation. The Step Refinement spec should add Form 3 only after N≥2.

**Why the counter PARTIALLY succeeds:** N=1 IS premature for prescriptive formalization. The spec shouldn't say "use Form 3 when X."

**Why the counter ultimately fails (structural grounds):** descriptive naming doesn't require N=many. The Form-3 instance is structurally distinct from Form 1 and Form 2 (rule integrated into spine procedure rather than placed at canonical home as a separate subsection). Without acknowledging Form 3, future contributors might either (a) lift the absorbed form unnecessarily, breaking what works; or (b) face ambiguity about where their content belongs. Acknowledging Form 3 prevents both failure modes. The acknowledgment is descriptive (just naming what's there), not prescriptive (no recommendation about when to use).

**Confidence:** HIGH that three-form framing is load-bearing.

**Resolution:** the three-form framing is admitted descriptively. Form 3 is named as "an absorbed-form pattern observed in at least one corpus instance" — acknowledgment without prescription.

**What is now fixed:** the Step Refinement spec gets a Three-Forms addendum.

**What is no longer allowed:** treating Form 2 (scattered) and Form 1 (standalone) as the same thing — they need different tidy treatments.

**What now depends on this choice:** per-discipline plans must tag candidates by form + sub-type.

**What changed in the conceptual model:** from "bolted-on rules with a 4-element shape" to "Step Refinements in three forms with the same 4-element shape, each form having different placement / lifting characteristics."

This isn't "the prior was wrong, throw it out." It's "the prior conflated two scopes; this inquiry separates them." Both readings are simultaneously correct: no machinery enforcement; active per-discipline tidying. Same principle, two layers.

**Confidence:** HIGH — the phase-fit principle is preserved; what's added is precision about its scope.

### Ambiguity 3: Is Form-2-to-Form-1 lifting a uniform operation?

**Why the counter SUCCEEDS:** the audit reveals 4 distinct Form-2 sub-types, each with a different lifting approach:

**Confidence:** HIGH that the operation is non-uniform; HIGH that the sub-types map to specific instances.

**Resolution:** Form-2-to-Form-1 lifting has 4 sub-types with named recipes. Per-discipline plans tag each candidate by sub-type so the lifting recipe is explicit.

**What is now fixed:** lifting sub-types are named.

**What is no longer allowed:** treating "tidy this discipline" as a uniform operation.
