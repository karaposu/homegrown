# Branch: Discipline Relevance Audit

## Question
Are the three non-core thinking disciplines (Exploration, Decomposition, Comprehension) still relevant to the system's goal of becoming a self-automated thinking system capable of covering the full thinking space — including hard problems like mathematical conjectures — and what role do they play alongside the established SIC loop and boundary disciplines (R+N)?

## Goal
A clear architectural verdict for each of the three disciplines: (a) is it a distinct cognitive operation the SIC loop cannot perform, (b) is it redundant with SIC and should be merged/killed, or (c) is it a specialized mode that SIC subsumes when configured properly? The user should know whether to invest in developing these three disciplines or focus entirely on deepening SIC+R+N. Additionally: does the current architecture (SIC+R+N + possibly these three) have structural gaps that prevent it from covering the full thinking space?

## Scope Check
Question covers goal: NO — the goal asks about structural gaps for full thinking-space coverage, but the question focuses only on the three disciplines' relevance. The aspiration toward consciousness and solving mathematical conjectures is a bigger question than "are these three files relevant."

Consider widening to: "What is the complete architecture needed for a self-automated thinking system that covers the full thinking space, and where do the existing disciplines (SIC, R, N, Exploration, Decomposition, Comprehension) fit within it?"
